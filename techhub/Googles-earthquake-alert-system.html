Google’s Android phone-powered earthquake alert system

Google has announced the development of an algorithm designed to detect earthquakes. the accelerometer in your Android phone will become one data point and eventually, that system will automatically send warnings to people who could be impacted.

It’s a feature made possible through domination of Android phones around the world and clever use of algorithms on big data. it shows that there are some other ways that smartphones could be used for something more important than doomscrolling.

Currently, Google is partnering with the United States Geological Survey and the California Office of Emergency Services to send the agencies’ earthquake alerts to Android users in that state. Those alerts are generated by the already-existing ShakeAlert system, which uses data generated by traditional seismometers.

Finally, once Google feels more confident about the accuracy of the system, it will begin actively sending out earthquake warnings to people who live in areas where there are not seismometer-based warning systems.
